CREATING WITH MACHINES

Youngjin Oh examines the mechanisms by which large language model AIs—such as Notion AI, Wordcraft, and ChatGPT—operate and make suggestions to users during the writing process. Stepping away from the current hype surrounding the popularization of AI, the work critically assesses how useful these tools truly are in actual creative practices. It also poses questions about what is necessary for the literature of the future to be augmented through the help of AI.
IT TAKES A WHOLE WORLD TO LIBERATE A SINGLE DESIGNER

Designer Ina Shin and editor Heewon Baek reflect on their journey of pursuing “design as a form of social action.” Shin shares her path from overcoming oppression and self-exploitation in the process of becoming a designer to practicing “transformative justice” as a politicized designer. Baek, drawing from her experience collaborating with designers, proposes a model of collaboration based on care and just relationships, while also discussing its experiments and limitations.

MEDIA, TECHNOLOGY, AND THE “POST-TESTIMONY” — PART 1

In the roundtable “Media, Technology, and the ‘Post-Testimony’,” the interactive testimony project Eternal Testimony on the Japanese military ‘comfort women’ was demonstrated and discussed with the panel. Together, they explored how the evolving form of technological media preserves “old testimonies,” while also confronting the risks of mummification and objectification. The discussion critically examined the enduring value of testimony that must be preserved despite such tensions.
